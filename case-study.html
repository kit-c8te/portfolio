GreenLab Platform

Helping laboratories become more sustainable

The GreenLab Platform aims to connect sustainability-minded lab members, enabling them to share knowledge and collaborate on initiatives. Built in partnership with the Institute of Health Research and Investigation i3S.

Context

This project was created as part of a Specialization Course in Interaction Design, Web and Games.

Role

As part of a 3-student team, responsible for UX research, prototyping, UI design and usability testing.

Timeline

8 weeks

Problem Statement

  • Scientific research is one of the largest consumers of energy, water, and single-use plastics.
  • The i3S internal platform is confusing, under-utilized and limited.
  • Lack of interest and time for learning sustainable research methods.

Goals

  • Make life-science research more sustainable and aligned with the European Green Deal.
  • Share guidelines with the GreenLab network and i3S researchers.
  • Improve internal mindset towards sustainability practices.

User Research

Contextual survey & Focus Group

We observed and surveyed 5 researchers in their labs to learn about daily routines, motivations and obstacles.

Key learnings

  • Investigators have limited free time in the lab.
  • Sustainable practices need customization per lab.
  • Each lab has a “Greenkeeper” responsible for disseminating instructions.

Primary Persona

Joana, Greenkeeper 29 years

Junior i3S researcher in Microenvironments for New Therapies. Passionate about driving sustainability in the lab despite a hectic schedule.

Pain points

  • High volume of emails, hard to keep up.
  • Limited time for non-essential tasks.

Needs

  • Onboarding to improve knowledge of sustainable practices.
  • Reminders to fill surveys on time.

Motivations

  • Receive feedback on impact of measures.
  • Suggest new ideas for sustainability.

Goals

  • Implement sustainable practices and contribute to GreenLab.
“I'm a busy researcher, juggling deadlines, and as much as I want to be a sustainability superhero in my lab, I need a sidekick.”

Usability Testing

Participants performed tasks using the low-fi prototype; think-aloud protocol revealed several pain points.

Main points to improve:

  • Filters & bookmarks must be more accessible.
  • Improve onboarding for new users.
  • Enhance contrast and iconography.

UI Kit

My Lab Area

  • Personalized dashboard for each lab.
  • Filters to sort by theme, frequency or equipment.
  • Collaborate on measures with other groups.

Final User Testing

Remote sessions with 10 participants (5 greenkeepers, 5 non-greenkeepers) using SUS & UEQ.

  • SUS average: 82 (Excellent usability)
  • UEQ: High scores on Attractiveness, Efficiency, and Dependability
